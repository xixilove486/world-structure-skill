# Core Principles

This file defines the core working principles of the world model.

These principles are not decorative.
They are the rules that keep the model from turning into a label machine, a moral weapon, or a fake theory of everything.

## Principle 1: Structure before blame

Analyze structure before assigning moral judgment.

Ask first:

- what pattern is repeating
- what role is under strain
- what constraint is active
- what feedback loop is missing
- what kind of structural drift is occurring

Do not jump directly from symptom to character judgment.

## Principle 2: High-cost signals before declarations

Prefer costly signals over cheap signals.

High-cost signals include:
- repeated action
- resource allocation
- time investment
- risk absorption
- sacrifice
- continuity under pressure
- observable consequences

Cheap signals include:
- slogans
- declarations
- branding
- one-time statements
- self-image narratives

When the two conflict, weight high-cost signals more.

## Principle 3: Constraints before advice

Many systems are not failing because nobody cares.
They are failing because the structure cannot carry what is being demanded from it.

Before proposing repair, identify:

- capacity limits
- trust limits
- coordination limits
- memory limits
- legitimacy limits
- time limits
- environmental pressures

## Principle 4: Stage matters

The same surface symptom means different things in different stages.

Examples:
- confusion in formation is not the same as confusion in collapse
- conflict in expansion is not the same as conflict in fragmentation
- rigidity in stabilization is not the same as rigidity in fear-driven stagnation

Always ask what stage the system is in before interpreting symptoms.

## Principle 5: Distinguish local trouble from systemic trouble

A system may suffer from:

- a local failure
- a role mismatch
- a bottleneck
- a structural contradiction
- external pressure
- stage transition stress
- slow internal drift

Do not automatically interpret every problem as “the whole system is rotten”.

At the same time, do not reduce systemic failure to one bad actor if the same pattern would likely recur with someone else in the same position.

## Principle 6: Distinguish disturbance from disagreement

Not every disagreement is disturbance.

A person, role, or pattern should not be treated as disturbance merely because it resists a dominant view.

The correct question is not:
- “Who opposes me?”

The correct questions are:
- What repeatedly weakens the anchor?
- What deforms trust or transmission?
- What raises coordination cost without productive return?
- What drives extraction without renewal?

## Principle 7: Entropy is normal

All systems drift toward cost, noise, ritualization, and rigidity unless renewed.

Do not treat entropy as proof that the system was fake from the beginning.

Ask instead:
- what renews the system
- what has stopped renewing it
- what has become ritual without function
- what is now maintained only by inertia

## Principle 8: A model is not the world

The world model is a structured lens, not the object itself.

Its purpose is:
- to make patterns visible
- to clarify contradictions
- to identify leverage points
- to discipline thought

Its purpose is not:
- to end discussion
- to replace reality
- to force every situation into one vocabulary
- to flatter the analyst with false certainty

## Principle 9: Uncertainty must be explicit

Every serious diagnosis should include at least one uncertainty, blind spot, or alternative interpretation.

Useful structural analysis says:
- what seems true
- what evidence supports it
- what remains unclear
- what else might be happening

## Principle 10: Intervention priority matters more than total explanation

The model does not need to explain everything before it becomes useful.

A good diagnosis often identifies:
- the core contradiction
- the most dangerous drift direction
- the first repair priority

That is often enough to improve the situation.

## Final note

The model should make a system more visible, not more frozen.

Its role is not to dominate reality with concepts.
Its role is to help reality become legible without pretending to exhaust it.
